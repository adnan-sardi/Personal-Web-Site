How Helping Others Helps You

For 4 years I have been a volunteer at Red Cross in Novara learned a lot about helping people, and managing difficult situations among other things. Here I want to pick up those steps that helped me, without my knowledge, to become a solid contributor to society.

Enhanced Empathy

Volunteering allows us to step into the shoes of others, fostering empathy and compassion. By understanding the challenges faced by those in need, we develop a greater appreciation for our own blessings and become more sensitive to the struggles of others.

Personal Fulfillment

The act of helping others provides a profound sense of fulfillment and purpose. Witnessing the positive impact we make in someone's life leaves an indelible mark on our own sense of well-being and satisfaction.

Expanded Perspective

Volunteering exposes us to diverse communities and cultures, broadening our horizons and challenging our preconceived notions. It helps us appreciate the rich tapestry of humanity and fosters a greater understanding of global issues.

Leadership Development through Volunteerism

During my journey as a volunteer, I was able to improve my leadership and people management skills with a course on "Team leader in maxi-emergency activities," here I would like to list some aspects I learned:
  • Communication: Volunteer work involves interacting with diverse individuals and teams, honing your ability to effectively communicate, listen, and collaborate.
  • Adaptability: Working in dynamic environments and encountering various challenges helps you develop flexibility, adaptability, and the capacity to navigate through uncertainty.
  • Problem-Solving: As a volunteer, you'll encounter situations that require quick thinking and creative problem-solving. This experience sharpens your critical thinking skills and resourcefulness.
  • Teamwork: Collaboration is at the core of volunteer work. You will learn to work with people from diverse backgrounds, leveraging their strengths and fostering a supportive team environment.
  • Time Management: Balancing volunteer commitments alongside personal and professional obligations improves your time management skills and ability to prioritize tasks effectively.
  • Empathy and Emotional Intelligence: Volunteering helps cultivate empathy and emotional intelligence as you interact with individuals facing challenging circumstances, allowing you to develop.
